Korean Classic

Crispy Seafood Scallion Pancake (Pajeon)

An authentic, extra-crispy savory pancake packed with fresh scallions and a rich seafood combination.

⏱️ Prep & Cook: 20 mins
⭐ Difficulty: Easy
👥 Servings: 2 servings

🛒 Essential Ingredients

  • Fresh Scallions (Green Onions): 1 large bunch (approx. 5-6 oz)
  • Assorted Seafood Mix: 1 cup
  • Korean Frying Mix (Buchimgaru): 1 cup
  • Korean Crispy Frying Mix (Twigimgaru): 1/2 cup
  • Ice-Cold Water: 1.2 cups
  • Large Egg (lightly beaten): 1
  • Red & Green Chili Peppers (sliced): 1 each (optional)
  • Vegetable Oil for frying: Generous amount
💡 Pro Tips for Ultimate Crispiness:
  • Always use ice-cold water! It slows down gluten development in the flour mix, resulting in a noticeably crispier, lighter crust.
  • Pat dry the seafood mix with paper towels thoroughly before adding it to prevent the pancake from getting soggy.

🍳 Step-by-Step Directions

1
Prep the Ingredients Thoroughly wash and completely dry the scallions. Cut them in half to fit your frying pan. Pat dry the seafood mix with paper towels to eliminate excess moisture (this prevents the pancake from becoming soggy).
2
Mix the Batter In a mixing bowl, combine 1 cup of Korean frying mix, 1/2 cup of crispy frying mix, and 1.2 cups of ice-cold water. Whisk lightly just until the flour is incorporated. Do not overmix, or the pancake will lose its crispy texture.
3
Assemble in the Pan Heat a generous amount of vegetable oil in a large non-stick skillet over medium-high heat. Arrange the scallions flat and parallel in the pan, then ladle the batter evenly over the scallions. Scatter the seafood mix and sliced chilis right on top.
4
Bind and Flip Pour the lightly beaten egg across the top surface (this acts as a delicious binder for the seafood). Cook until the bottom turns beautifully golden-brown and crispy. Carefully flip, drizzle a touch more oil around the edges, and cook until both sides are perfectly crispy. Serve hot with a soy dipping sauce!
