10-Min Super Easy Home Cook

Pork Belly & Bean Sprouts (Daepae-Samgyeopsal Sukju-Bokkeum)

Crispy thin pork belly slices stir-fried with crunchy mung bean sprouts in a savory garlic-oyster glaze. Extremely addictive!

⚡ Super Fast Steps:
  1. Pan-fry 200g of thin pork belly with sliced garlic till golden brown. Remove excess oil.
  2. Add 1 tbsp soy sauce, 1 tbsp oyster sauce, and 1/2 tbsp sugar over the meat.
  3. Throw in a big handful of fresh bean sprouts (Sukju) and stir-fry on high heat for just 1 minute.
  4. Turn off the heat, drizzle sesame oil, and top with black pepper!
🍺
K-Food Insider Tip: This dish is a legendary match with 'Somaek' (a classic Korean cocktail of Soju mixed with cold beer). The rich, crispy pork and refreshing ice-cold drink create an absolutely heavenly combination!

Easy Side Dishes

5-Minute Authentic Banchan

Korean meals are never complete without 'Banchan' (side dishes). Here are the easiest, most delicious side dishes you can duplicate at home in minutes!

7-Min Microwave Steamed Egg (Gyeran-Jjim)

Super fluffy, pudding-like soft savory egg dish made purely in a microwave.

⚡ 4-Step Recipe:
  1. Crack 2 eggs into a rice-bowl-sized bowl.
  2. Whisk thoroughly with chopsticks.
  3. Pour 100ml of water and add a pinch of salt.
  4. Microwave for about 7 minutes and serve hot!

Garlic Sesame Bean Sprouts (Sukju-Namul)

Crunchy, nutty, and incredibly refreshing side dish that cleanses your palate.

⚡ 4-Step Recipe:
  1. Blanch mung bean sprouts in boiling water for 1-2 mins.
  2. Rinse in ice-cold water and squeeze out moisture.
  3. Toss with minced garlic, salt, and sesame oil.
  4. Garnish with toasted sesame seeds and enjoy.

Sweet & Soy Braised Potatoes (Gamja-Jorim)

Soft, cubed potatoes glazed in a delicious sweet and savory soy sauce reduction.

⚡ 4-Step Recipe:
  1. Cube 2 medium potatoes and rinse to remove starch.
  2. Pan-fry cubes with 1 tbsp of oil for 3 mins.
  3. Pour 1/2 cup water, 3 tbsp soy sauce, 2 tbsp sugar.
  4. Simmer over medium-low heat until the sauce reduces.

K-Comfort Soup

Authentic Fish Cake Soup (Eomuk-Tang)

Warm up your soul with comforting, street-style skewered fish cake soup.

Easy Street-Style Recipe

  1. Boil the Broth: Boil 6 cups of water with anchovy & kelp packs and big slices of Korean radish for 15-20 minutes.
  2. Prepare Fish Cakes: Skewer your Korean fish cakes in a wavy pattern, or cut them into bite-sized pieces.
  3. Seasoning: Remove the soup packs. Add 1 tbsp of soy sauce, 1 tbsp of tuna sauce (or salt), and minced garlic.
  4. Simmer & Serve: Put the fish cakes into the boiling broth and simmer for 5 minutes. Top with sliced scallions and red pepper!

Korean Classic Crispy Pancake

Crispy Seafood Scallion Pancake (Haemul-Pajeon)

Secret Crispy Recipe Guide:

  • Mix the Batter: Combine 1 cup of Korean pancake mix (Buchimgaru) with 3/4 cup of ice-cold water. Cold water is the key to making it super crunchy!
  • Arrange Scallions: Heat plenty of oil in a pan, place a handful of whole scallions down flat, and pour the batter evenly over them.
  • Add Seafood: Place mixed seafood (shrimp, squid, clams) on top of the batter, then add a beaten egg over the seafood to lock everything in place.
  • Flip to Perfection: Fry until the bottom is deeply golden brown, flip it carefully, and press down. Serve instantly with soy sauce dipping!
